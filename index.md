I am a PhD candidate in Fluid Mechanics at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), focusing on computational fluid dynamics (CFD), reduced-order modeling (ROM), and machine-learning-enhanced simulation methods for liquid metal-cooled fast reactor applications.

My research combines projection-based ROM (POD-Galerkin), neural-network-based surrogate modeling, physics-informed learning, and neural operator concepts to accelerate high-fidelity simulation workflows while preserving relevant physics for engineering analysis.

## Research Focus

- Reduced-order modeling for Navier-Stokes and turbulence-resolved workflows
- Domain decomposition and local reduced subspaces for repeating subdomains
- Machine learning and neural networks for data-driven surrogate modeling
- Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), DeepONet, and neural operator methods
- Simplified high-fidelity CFD strategies for complex bundle geometries

## Highlights

- Investigated distances between POD reduced subspaces of repeating subdomains and their impact on local modeling strategies.
- Developed expertise in machine learning, neural networks, PINNs, and neural operator approaches for physics-aware surrogate modeling.
- Contributed to ongoing work on coupling-efficient ROM pipelines for large-scale reactor-related geometries.
